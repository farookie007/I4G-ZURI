The Legend of KOBE BRYANT, the highschool kid who challenged the gods of the game

On January 26, 2020; as the world was battling with the Covid-19 pandemic, the world of sports received a devastating news of the death of basketball legend, KOBE BRYANT. He was going to see a basketball match of his daughter when their helicopter crashed which cost the lives of everyone onboard including his 13 year old daughter, Gigi. The death was such a tragedy as there were seven other passengers with them. He died at the age of 41 and was survived by two daughters and a wife.

Kobe Bean Bryant was a professional American Basketball player and the shooting guard of the Los Angeles Lakers team in the NBA. He's arguably one of the best players to ever play the game of basketball, his life was a memorable one as he was priviledged to play with many other great names in the league. He loved the competition that comes with every game and rather than feeling intimidated as a rookie, he faced the challenges heads-on. He played with so much determination and focus that he was never looked down upon as a rookie, during his prime and until his last professional game in which he scored a whooping 60 points.

He was one of the greatest players of the L.A Lakers. Despite having great players like Shaquille O'Neal on his team, he has the most appearances in the NBA as a starter. He has played with other great players like Michael Jordan, Shaquille O'Neal, Yao Ming, Lebron James, Ray Allen, e.t.c. He was the first player to ever dunk on Houston Rockets' center, Yao Ming who has a record of not ever being postered. Also the second player with highest points in a single NBA game with 81 points, second to Wilt Chamberlain's 100 points game.

He is also famous for his discipline and not celebrating early victories as he said in one of his interviews, Despite fear, finish the job. He also gave himself the name 'Black Mamba' and the popular saying of Black Mamba Mentality which means never giving up.
